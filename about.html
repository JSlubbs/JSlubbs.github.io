Background

The work has always been about making complexity legible.

I started in consulting, doing UX across fintech, healthcare, and industrial clients. Then enterprise SEO at Conductor, where I got comfortable with dense data, large systems, and research-driven product work. Then Visor, where I was the only designer for four years and built everything from a nearly blank Figma file.

The through-line has been low-convention problems: spaces where the design patterns don't exist yet and the work is partly figuring out what the right questions are. That's what drew me to MCP Manager, and it's what drew me to apply here.

I have a B.A. in Psychology from Baruch College (CUNY), which turns out to be a surprisingly useful foundation for design work. Understanding how people build mental models, where attention goes, what makes an interface feel trustworthy or confusing — a lot of that is just applied cognitive psychology.

How I work

Close to the models, close to the code.

I work regularly with Claude, Replit, and v0. I'm interested in what it means to design for and with AI systems, not just to make interfaces that sit in front of them. MCP Manager is a good example: the interesting design problem isn't the UI, it's the mental model. How do you make an invisible infrastructure layer visible and governable without overwhelming the people who have to use it?

I prototype in code when it's faster than Figma. I maintain my own design system files alongside the products I build. I ship documentation and specs that don't require me to be in the room.

Outside work

Other things that take up space.

It's hard not to notice that once you've lived inside a few of them.
